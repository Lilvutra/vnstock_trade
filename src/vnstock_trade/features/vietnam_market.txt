
> Vietnam has hard price limits (±7% HOSE, ±10% HNX).
> Why does this matter for ML features?
> And how can it create real alpha?

---

# 🇻🇳 1️⃣ What Hard Price Limits Do Structurally

On HOSE:

* Stock cannot move more than ±7% per day.

This creates:

* Artificial truncation of returns
* Order book locking at ceiling/floor
* Multi-day continuation patterns
* Liquidity distortion

This is not present in US markets.

That’s why features exploiting this can be powerful.

---

# 🔥 2️⃣ Why Price Limits Create Predictable Behavior

In a free market:

If real fair value jumps +15%, price immediately adjusts.

In Vietnam:

If fair value jumps +15%:

Day 1 → +7% (limit up)
Day 2 → +7%
Day 3 → +1%

Price discovery is slowed.

That creates **multi-day momentum spillover**.

This is structural — not just statistical.

---

# 📈 3️⃣ Ceiling Lock Phenomenon

When a stock:

* Hits +7%
* Has huge unmatched buy volume at ceiling
* Zero sellers

It becomes “locked”.

Retail psychology kicks in:

* FOMO
* Social media hype
* Herd behavior

Often leads to:

2–5 day continuation waves.

Feature idea:

```
Consecutive_limit_up_days
Unmatched_ceiling_volume_ratio
```

---

# 📉 4️⃣ Floor Lock and Panic Cascades

Same on downside:

When stock hits -7% and locks:

* No buyers
* Huge sell queue

This creates:

Forced liquidation cascade
Margin call spillover
Liquidity vacuum

Often continues next day.

Feature idea:

```
Consecutive_floor_days
Sell_queue_ratio
```

---

# 🧠 5️⃣ Why ML Loves This

Because limits create:

Nonlinear regime behavior.

Normal markets:

Returns roughly continuous.

Vietnam:

Returns are censored.

Distribution looks like:

Big spikes at +7% and -7%.

This changes statistical structure.

Models that ignore this miss signal.

---

# 🎯 6️⃣ Hidden Dynamic: Supply–Demand Imbalance Memory

When stock closes at ceiling:

Unfilled demand carries to next day.

There is pent-up pressure.

Unlike normal markets where pressure clears instantly.

That creates autocorrelation.

This is not classic momentum.

It is constrained price discovery.

---

# 📊 7️⃣ Feature Engineering Ideas Around Limits

### 🔹 Limit Proximity Feature

Distance to upper limit at close.

If stock closes at +6.8%, strong probability of hitting +7% next day.

---

### 🔹 Ceiling Volume Imbalance

Unmatched buy volume at ceiling / total volume.

Large ratio → strong continuation.

---

### 🔹 Exhaustion Signal

3–4 consecutive limit-ups + shrinking volume
→ blow-off top risk.

---

### 🔹 Liquidity Drying Feature

Volume collapses while price at limit
→ fragility increases.

---

# 🧨 8️⃣ Why This Can Be “Killer” Alpha

Because:

Price limits distort equilibrium adjustment.

This is mechanical inefficiency.

Not just statistical noise.

Emerging markets with limits:

* China A-shares
* Vietnam
* Some frontier markets

Often show similar continuation behavior.

---

# ⚠️ But Be Careful

Limits also create:

* Fake breakouts
* Illiquid traps
* Exit difficulty

Backtests must include:

* Realistic fill assumption
* Slippage
* Queue position logic

Many strategies look great because they assume instant execution at ceiling.

In reality:
You may never get filled.

---

# 🔥 Why Developed Markets Don’t Have This Edge

US markets rarely use daily hard limits on individual stocks.

So:

Price discovery faster
Less multi-day artificial continuation

Vietnam structure creates edge.
